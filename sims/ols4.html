Fit a Model by Choosing Two Points

This app lets you define a line by clicking two observed points. The app then compares your model with the ordinary least squares model, the orthogonal-fit model, and the mean-of-y model.

Observed Data and Competing Models

Help with Interpretation

Main idea: Different fitted lines imply different residual patterns and different sums of squared error. This app lets you compare those directly.

What to do: First look at the cloud of points. Then click two observed points to define your own model. Compare its fit with the OLS model and the orthogonal-fit model.

What to look for: The OLS model minimizes squared vertical distances. The orthogonal-fit model instead uses perpendicular distances to the line. The mean-of-y model shows total vertical variation in y.

Attenuation idea: When x contains measurement error, the OLS slope is often pulled toward zero. Comparing the OLS model with the orthogonal-fit model helps make that visible.
