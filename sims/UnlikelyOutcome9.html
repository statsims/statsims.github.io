Help with Interpretation

How to use this app: Start with the observed groups and look at the observed difference in means. Then use the step buttons to move through the shuffle logic. After that, add many shuffles to the null distribution.

Main purpose: This app is designed to show how unusual the observed result would be if the null idea were true that group membership does not matter.

Three stages: First, inspect the observed grouping. Second, pool the values together to represent the null world where labels do not matter. Third, deal one shuffled grouping and compare its difference with the observed result.

Fixed-point idea: The points do not move in the shuffle panel. Their values stay fixed. Only the labels change. That is the key idea behind a permutation test.

Percentile framing: This app shows where the observed result sits in the shuffle distribution. You can ask: what percentile is this result, and how many shuffled outcomes are as extreme or more extreme?

Reference percentiles: For a two-sided rule, the app marks the 2.5th and 97.5th percentiles. For one-sided rules, it marks the 5th or 95th percentile. These are reference points from the shuffle distribution, not formal critical values.

Card-shuffle metaphor: Imagine writing all observed outcomes on cards. Under the null, the cards could have been dealt to either group. Relabel the cards, redeal, and compare the new difference with the observed one.
