Help with Interpretation

Main idea: A DAG implies which paths create bias and which controls make sense. The simulations then show how those structural choices affect the treatment slope in regression.

What to do: Click variables to condition on them. Compare the unadjusted slope, the adjusted slope, and the true structural effect built into the data-generating process.

Why the true effect matters: Statistical estimates can move around for many reasons. Bias becomes easiest to understand when you can compare an estimate with the true effect used to generate the simulated data.

Target effect matters too: A total effect and a direct effect are not the same thing. Conditioning on a mediator might be correct for one question and incorrect for another.

Sampling distribution view: Repeated simulations show that bias shifts the center of the slope distribution, not just one estimate on one dataset.
