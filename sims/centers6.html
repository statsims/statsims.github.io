Getting Started

  1. Choose a preset dataset from the right panel. Each one illustrates a different statistical story.
  2. Click the chart to add your own values; click any dot to remove it.
  3. Use the concept tabs below the chart to explore each idea in depth.
  4. The live stats panel on the right always reflects the current data.
  5. Try the Outliers tab to add extreme values and watch the mean get pulled while the median holds steady.

Key Concepts

  • Mean: The balance point. Sensitive to every value, especially outliers.
  • Median: The middle position. Resistant to outliers and skew.
  • Mode: The most frequent value. Works on any data type.
  • Spread: How dispersed data is around the center. Range, IQR, and Std Dev each tell a different part of the story.
  • Skew: When mean and median diverge, the data has a long tail in one direction.

Reading the Chart

  • Blue dashed line: The mean (x-bar)
  • Green solid line: The median
  • Shaded box: The IQR, the middle 50% of data (Q1 to Q3)
  • Dashed whiskers: Extend to min and max
  • Blue dots: Typical data values
  • Red dots: Statistical outliers (beyond 1.5 x IQR from the box)

Full Reference

Formulas and definitions for all measures.

Measures of Center
MeanSum of all values divided by n. The arithmetic average and balance point.
x-bar = (sum of xi) / n
MedianMiddle value when sorted. Resistant to outliers.
P50 = middle of sorted data
ModeMost frequently occurring value(s). Only measure that works for categorical data.
Harmonic MeanBest for rates and ratios (speeds, efficiencies).
H = n / sum(1/xi)
Measures of Spread
RangeMax minus min. Simple but sensitive to outliers.
Range = max - min
IQRSpread of the middle 50%. Robust to outliers.
IQR = Q3 - Q1
Std DevAverage squared distance from the mean, square-rooted.
s = sqrt(sum((xi - x-bar)^2) / n)
MADMedian of all absolute distances from the median. Robust to outliers.
MAD = median(|xi - M|)
Robust Alternatives
Trimmed MeanDrop the top and bottom p%, then average the rest. Used in Olympic judging.
mean of middle (1-2p)% of values
Winsorized MeanReplace extremes with the nearest non-extreme value, then average.
clip at Q[p] and Q[1-p], then mean
Weighted MeanEach value has a weight reflecting its importance (GPA, polls).
w-bar = sum(wi * xi) / sum(wi)
Key Rules
Outlier fenceQ1 - 1.5*IQR to Q3 + 1.5*IQR
Normal rule68% within +/-1s, 95% within +/-2s, 99.7% within +/-3s
Skew signalmean > median = right-skewed tail; mean < median = left-skewed tail
MAD to sigmaFor normal data: s ≈ 1.4826 * MAD
