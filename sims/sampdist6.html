How to read this

Population plot: This is the full population you are sampling from. The vertical dashed line marks μ.

Sampling distribution: Each value in this histogram is a sample mean from one repeated sample of size n.

As n increases: the spread of the sampling distribution usually shrinks, because the standard error gets smaller.

With skewed populations: the sampling distribution can still become more normal as repeated sample means accumulate, especially for larger n.
